And Then She Was Gone

Paper | Fall 2017 | Life-size replication

And Then She Was Gone memorializes the life of an important figure in my childhood and investigates how people choose to remember the things they have lost. The piece begins with the tale of a butterfly - a life so fleeting and a presence so delicate and fragile it's hard to even notice. It seems that we don't take time to observe its flight or consider how much time it may have left. We only notice its absence when it leaves us, when we can feel that it is gone.

This concept of ephemerality in the piece is contrasted with a simultaneous fight for preservation. What do we choose to hold onto? Beauty, grace, peformance - these ideas I explore through the metaphor of the dancer. The dancer's job is to perform, to dazzle with her aura. She endures mangled feet and painful practice to do so, but she protects us from this. In the end, we remember only her beauty and her grace - that is all she has allowed us to see.
